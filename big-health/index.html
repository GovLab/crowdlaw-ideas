Identifying the opportunities and challenges of using

BIG HEALTH DATA

WHAT IS
BIG HEALTH DATA?

Big health data refers to the ability to gather and analyze large quantities of information about health, wellness and lifestyle. Big health data includes information from health care providers as well as data from sources such as apps that track our sleep and exercise habits and the purchases we make.

Doctors, healthcare organizations, insurance companies, financial service providers, product and service companies and governments at every level are keen to use such data.

The Good News?

More use and sharing of data could enable better diagnoses, more targeted prevention and treatments, faster research and cures, creation of new tools to help us make healthier choices and economic growth from the creation of health data businesses.

The Bad News?

The collection, sharing and use of big health data could reveal sensitive personal information over which we have little control. This data can be sold without our consent. It can be used by entities for surveillance or discrimination, rather than to promote well-being.
